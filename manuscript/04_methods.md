# Methods

## Setup and notation

Within a single commuting-zone window $W$ (a polygon, projected to local UTM), let
$X = \{x_1,\dots,x_n\}$ be the open hospital locations falling in $W$. Let
$p(u)$ be the population surface (LandScan ambient, or ACS residential)
evaluated at location $u \in W$. We ask whether $X$ is consistent with a point
process whose first-order intensity is proportional to population.

## The population-proportional intensity

We define the null intensity as population, rescaled so its integral over the
window equals the observed hospital count:

$$
\lambda(u) \;=\; n \,\frac{p(u)}{\int_W p(v)\,dv}, \qquad u \in W .
$$

By construction $\int_W \lambda(u)\,du = n$, so a process drawn from $\lambda$
produces $n$ hospitals in expectation and the inhomogeneous K-function below is
directly comparable to its theoretical Poisson value $\pi r^2$. Because
$\lambda$ is **supplied by the raster**, there is no kernel bandwidth to choose
and no leave-one-out intensity to floor — the entire instability class that
arises when $\lambda$ is estimated from sparse points is absent. A small
positive floor is applied only to raster cells with zero ambient population that
nonetheless contain a hospital (e.g., an industrial-zoned cell), to keep
$1/\lambda$ finite; its level is reported and varied as a sensitivity check.

## The inhomogeneous K-function

We use the inhomogeneous K-function [@baddeley_inhomogeneous_2000], the
second-order summary reweighted by the (here, externally supplied) intensity:

$$
\hat{K}_{\text{inhom}}(r) \;=\; \frac{1}{|W|}
\sum_{i}\sum_{j \neq i}
\frac{\mathbf{1}\{\lVert x_i - x_j \rVert \le r\}\, e(x_i, x_j)}
     {\lambda(x_i)\,\lambda(x_j)} ,
$$

with $e(\cdot,\cdot)$ the Ripley isotropic edge-correction weight. Under the
null that hospitals follow population, $K_{\text{inhom}}(r) \approx \pi r^2$.
Values **above** $\pi r^2$ indicate hospitals are *more* clustered than
population warrants at scale $r$ (over-concentration); values **below** indicate
a more regular-than-population arrangement.

## The null and the Monte-Carlo envelope

The reference distribution is generated by simulating hospital patterns from the
population-proportional inhomogeneous Poisson process, $\text{rpoispp}(\lambda)$,
and recomputing $\hat{K}_{\text{inhom}}$ for each. With `nsim` simulations the
pointwise envelope is the simulation-wise min/max band (matching `spatstat`'s
default `nrank = 1`); a radius $r$ is flagged **over-concentrated** where the
observed curve exceeds the upper envelope and **dispersed** where it falls below
the lower envelope. Default parameters: `nsim = 999`, an $r$ grid in meters
appropriate to metro scale, Ripley isotropic edge correction, fixed `seed` for
reproducibility, with independent L'Ecuyer-CMRG streams across simulations.

The first-order intensity $\lambda$ is identical in the observed statistic and
in every simulation (it is the population surface, not re-estimated per
simulation), so the test conditions cleanly on population and isolates the
*second-order* (clustering) question.

variants address capacity:

## Global (family-wise) envelope test across commuting zones

Running a pointwise envelope in each of several hundred commuting zones invites
multiple-comparison inflation. We therefore apply a **global envelope test**
(the Myllymäki et al. extreme-rank-length, ERL, construction)
[@myllymaki_global_2017] so that a zone's "significant" verdict controls the
family-wise error rate over the $r$ grid, and we report the distribution of
global $p$-values across zones.

## Local decomposition (where the deviation is)

The global $K_{\text{inhom}}$ is an average over the window; it states *that* a
CBSA departs from proportionality, not *where*. We decompose it with the local
inhomogeneous K-function, which yields one local contribution per hospital (and,
on the complementary side, a population-weighted coverage map of where people
sit far from any hospital relative to expectation). This converts a global
verdict into an interpretable map of over-served cores and under-served gaps.

## Contrast design (ambient vs. residential)

Every quantity above is computed twice — once with the LandScan ambient
$\lambda$, once with the ACS residential $\lambda$ — holding the hospital
pattern, window, edge correction, $r$ grid, and seeds fixed. The reported
contrast is the set of CBSAs whose verdict differs between the two demand
surfaces.

## Implementation and reproducibility

The reference engine is **R / `spatstat`** (`Kinhom`, `envelope`, `rpoispp` on a
pixel-image intensity, `localKinhom`, and the `GET` package for the global
test); an independent **Python** port (`numpy`/`scipy`/`shapely`/`geopandas`,
adapted from the validated Kcross port) serves as a cross-check. Each run writes
the envelope curve, a verdict, and a JSON metadata record (counts, parameters,
projection, runtime). Code is in `code/` and is not part of the submitted
manuscript.
